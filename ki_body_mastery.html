Simple Food Combining for Optimal Digestion

One of the first things that my mentor, Dr. Theresa Dale taught me was the principles of proper food combining. As she walked me through the basic rules, my mind was truly blown. Here was a way to make sure the proteins digested well, and didn't putrefy the liver.

I had learned in Naturopathic school that fermented animal protein created 10 different toxic gases, one of which I still recall: Cadaverine! So I knew it was important to digest cleanly. However, they did not teach food combining at that institution, so thank goodness I ended up working with Theresa on my health.

The rules are simple, but fly in the face of modern eating. In proper food combining we do not combine protein with starches/carbs/sugars, as they always get broken down first. The enzyme needed for protein, called protease, is a little more challenging for the pancreas, so it gets secreted later. This gives the simple sugars a chance to start fermenting our protein — which is exactly what happens — then toxic gases form, go out into our bloodstreams, and our poor liver has to neutralize them.

What to combine with your protein: Always with vegetables, and I don't mean potatoes, as they are a starch! Always combine protein with a little healthy fat — such as virgin olive oil based salad dressing, avocado, coconut oil or grass-fed butter. Use low-sugar veggies such as greens, asparagus, broccoli, etc.

Another huge controversy: either eat fruit alone, with no other type of food, or leave it alone! Then combine starches (complex carbs) with vegetables and a little healthy fat.

The next rule: do NOT drink beverages with the meal, as it directly dilutes your digestive enzymes, which are also in liquid form. Dr. Dale insisted I drink all of my water in between meals, and had me set a goal of at least 8 ten-ounce glasses of pure water daily.

The rules in a short list:

  • Combine protein with veggies and healthy fats — no starch or carbs!
  • Combine Starches/Carbs with veggies and healthy fats.
  • No fruit with meals; either eat it alone or leave it alone.
  • No beverages with meals except to take your supplements.
  • Hydrate thoroughly in between your meals. Aim for 80 fluid ounces or more. Do not drink tap water.

Let's Talk About Eating…

One of the more important things to consider when wanting to upgrade our energy and wellbeing is HOW we eat. Not just what we eat, but the actual act of eating itself.

Growing up, in our family we raised multiple animals — from goats, pigs, dogs, cats, and horses. They all ate exactly the same way: deliberately, intentionally and undistracted from the act. They chewed, lots, and seemed serene while doing it.

Contrast that image with our contemporary scene for humans eating. It seems a little insane, for many, as we are multi-tasking, watching TV or on our smart phones; barely chewing and washing our mouthful down with a beverage. Speaking of mouthful, often the size of the bite is super large, and if we don't proceed to chew it well, it won't actually digest well.

When we put food in our mouth and start to chew it, our salivary glands get activated, and a real-time communication occurs between those glands and the pancreas. The glands inform the pancreas which types of enzymes will be needed for what is coming down the pike. Proteins require different enzymes than fats or carbs. Failing to chew properly is a recipe for disaster, as the pancreas doesn't get the clear signals, and now instead of fuel, that food becomes a stress for the body.

So chewing well is intelligent. NOT chewing well is essentially saying NO to life in a real way. It is a fact that the first place nutritional deficiencies show up in the body is in the mental/cognitive function of the brain! Let's eat intelligently!
